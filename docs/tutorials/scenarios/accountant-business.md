# Scenario · Accountant ↔ Small business — books without attachments

**Hook.** "Your accountant has your bank login. Read that sentence again. Here's the version where they get a folder of receipts for exactly one month, and you get a bookkeeper you can question at 11pm."

1. **Pair** — one invite link over any chat.
2. **Business side** (wizard → "I'm the business owner") — one step: share the invoices folder, read-only, **for a month**. The countdown is visible from second one; when it hits zero the access is gone without anyone remembering to remove it.
3. **Accountant side** (wizard → "I'm the accountant") — create the *bookkeeper* assistant (its folder is where client docs arrive), share it back.
4. **The payoff** — the business owner asks the 🤖 bookkeeper: *"what did we spend on software subscriptions in June?"* Answer with the source file cited, straight from the accountant's machine.
5. **The wow** — month ends on camera (or *Stop* clicked): the folder share self-destructs, the audit shows `session-close (ttl expired)`. "Next month is a new share. Access never outlives the engagement."

**Recap.** No bank logins, no attachments, an assistant instead of email ping-pong, and access that expires like milk.
