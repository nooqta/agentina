# Scenario · IT helper ↔ Family or friend — help for an hour, watched, then over

**Hook.** "When you fix your mom's computer remotely, the tool you use takes over her whole screen and she trusts you with everything. She shouldn't have to — even with you. Here's help with a scope and a timer."

**The broadest-reach scenario: one side is genuinely non-technical — so their entire setup is a handful of taps, all guided by the app itself.**

1. **Pair** — the helper sends the invite; mom pastes it into the box. (Film the real thing: the onboarding screen has exactly two buttons; there is nothing to get wrong. If her machine needs a network address, the invite screen's yellow card fixes it in one tap.)
2. **Mom's one flow** — contact → *Share something* → **A folder** → the folder with the problem (picked from Home / Documents / Desktop suggestions — no typing paths) → **Read & write** ("they can also make changes — still only here") → **1 hour** ("quick help — gone before dinner") → the confirm card → *Share it*. The card literally spells out: read & write, self-destructs after 1 hour, stop anytime.
3. **The helper works** — asks the folder chip to `list`, reads the broken config, fixes what's needed — **only inside that folder**. A hop to `../` fails on camera; show the red line appear in mom's Activity feed the same second, in a plain sentence she can read.
4. **The wow** — the countdown hits zero mid-sentence: the helper's next action bounces with a red ⛔. Nothing to revoke, nothing to remember, nothing standing. Mom never shared her screen, her password, or anything outside one folder.

**Recap.** Scope + timer + audit = help without handing over your machine. *(This is the video most likely to travel outside tech circles.)*
