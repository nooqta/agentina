# Scenario · IT helper ↔ Family or friend — help for an hour, watched, then over

**Hook.** "When you fix your mom's computer remotely, the tool you use takes over her whole screen and she trusts you with everything. She shouldn't have to — even with you. Here's help with a scope and a timer."

**The broadest-reach scenario: one side is genuinely non-technical — so their entire setup is THREE clicks.**

1. **Pair** — the helper sends the invite; mom pastes it into the box. (Film the real thing: the console's onboarding screen has exactly two choices; there is nothing to get wrong.)
2. **Mom's one step** (wizard → "I'm the person being helped") — *share the folder with the problem, for 1 hour*. The folder comes from a picker showing Home / Documents / Desktop — no typing paths. The step card literally says "read & write, for 1 hour, then it stops itself."
3. **The helper works** — asks the 📁 share to `list`, reads the broken config, fixes what's needed — **only inside that folder**. A hop to `../` fails on camera; show the red line appear in mom's Activity feed the same second.
4. **The wow** — the countdown hits zero mid-sentence: the helper's next action bounces with a red ⛔. Nothing to revoke, nothing to remember, nothing standing. Mom never shared her screen, her password, or anything outside one folder.

**Recap.** Scope + timer + audit = help without handing over your machine. *(This is the video most likely to travel outside tech circles — the 'Mac Mini moment' candidate.)*
